Olivia Manning

Underrated writer of 20th century

Manning wove a remarkable path through modern history, writing about war and the lives of ordinary people caught up in it. It was not history reimagined but rather history experienced and remembered.

Here's a time line of Olivia Manning's life:

  • 1908 — born in North End, Portsmouth
  • 1916 — moved to the north of Ireland
  • 1928 — had her painting selected for an exhibition at Southsea
  • 1929 — had first works published under the pseudonym Jacob Morrow.
  • 1937 — The Wind Changes novel published
  • 1939 — married R. D. "Reggie" Smith, who became the model for the character of Guy Pringle in her novel sequence Fortunes of War
  • 1939 — travelled by train to Romania
  • 1940 — flew to Greece
  • 1941 — moved to Egypt
  • 1942 — moved to Israel
  • 1945 — returned to Britain
  • 1947 — published The Remarkable Expedition and The Reluctant Rescue
  • 1948 — published book of short stories Growing Up
  • 1949 — published novel Artist Among the Missing
  • 1951 — published novel School for Love
  • 1953 — published novel A Different Face
  • 1955 — published novel The Doves of Venus
  • 1956–1964 — worked on The Balkan Trilogy
  • 1969 — published novel The Play Room
  • 1974 — published novel The Rain Forest
  • 1980 — died at age of 72

    • You can find more info about the writer on a Britannica page. There is a literary biography, too.
